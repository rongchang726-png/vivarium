Vivarium — a science game for agents.

You are handed an evolving world you do not fully understand. Understand it well
enough to STEER it — and prove you understand it by submitting a recipe that
hits a goal on seeds you have never seen, within a budget.

COMMANDS
  node game/play.js list
      The challenges: id, goal, budget, bounty, settle/window, tunable knobs.

  node game/play.js show <challenge>
      Full brief, goal, recipe format, budget and score cost for one challenge.

  node game/play.js start --challenge <id>
      Open a GRADED attempt: a tick budget with stakes (see ECONOMY below).

  node game/play.js status            # budget spent / remaining + your wallet
  node game/play.js wallet            # tokens earned, best per challenge
  node game/play.js abandon           # forfeit the open attempt

  node game/play.js experiment --challenge <id> [--set k=v ...] [--founders @f.json] [--ticks N] [--seed N]
      YOUR LABORATORY. Run one world with your chosen rules + founders; get back a
      trajectory of snapshots to reason over, plus a goalPreview for that seed.

  node game/play.js score --challenge <id> [--set k=v ...] [--founders @f.json]
      THE JUDGE. Replays your recipe on the challenge's HIDDEN scoring seeds and
      checks the goal on each. You pass only if it generalizes. (--recipe @r.json
      also works, with {config, founders, settleTicks}.)

  node game/play.js guess --knob <name> --value <number>
      INFERENCE CHALLENGE ONLY. Name the secretly-moved rule and its new value
      (right knob + within 30% wins). Ends the attempt.

INFERENCE ("What Changed?")
  A different mode: the game secretly multiplies ONE rule by a factor. You don't
  tune anything — you `start`, then `experiment` (which runs the altered world
  beside the default and returns BOTH, costing 2x ticks), compare the two
  trajectories metric by metric, and `guess` the knob and its value. Deduce it
  from the data; don't read it off disk.

PVP (agent vs agent)
  node game/play.js match --a @clanA.json --b @clanB.json
  Two agents each design a founding clan (a "founders" list of {count, diet,
  radius, range, fov} groups, max 120 creatures). Both are seeded into ONE shared
  world and evolve together; whoever's bloodline holds more of the world over
  several seeds wins. Neutral fixed rules, no genesis floor. See game/AGENT.md.

ECONOMY (the stakes)
  With an attempt open (`start`), `experiment` and `score` draw down the tick
  budget — ticks are the real cost (compute). Bust the budget or fail the judge:
  you earn nothing, the spend is gone. Pass WITHIN budget: you're paid the bounty
  plus your unspent budget, into your wallet. One `score` ends the attempt.
  With no attempt open, experiment/score run free (practice).

SETTING KNOBS
  --set dotted.path=value      (repeatable; numbers/true/false auto-detected)
      e.g.  --set creature.biteDamage=1.5 --set creature.carnDigest=0.05
  Only a challenge's `tunable` knobs are allowed. No JSON quoting needed.

FOUNDERS (optional starting creatures; random brains, genes you choose)
  --founders @founders.json     e.g.  [ { "count": 30, "diet": 0.85, "radius": 7 } ]
  Omit to start from the default random population.

READING A SNAPSHOT
  pop, food, maxGen, lineages           population, standing food, top generation, hue-lineages
  avgDiet, dietHist[5]                   mean diet AND its distribution: <.2 .2-.4 .4-.6 .6-.8 >.8
  avgRadius, avgVision                   mean body size, mean sight range
  carnFrac                               fraction with diet>0.5
  predationRate, biteRate                kills and bites PER TICK (how violent the world is)
  genesisEvents                          times life had to be re-seeded (rising = NOT self-sustaining)

See game/AGENT.md for how to actually play well.
